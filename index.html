what do these numbers mean?
📈
Return 16–17%/yr: how fast your money compounds
₹10,000 put in today becomes roughly ₹47K–₹50K in 10 years at this rate — without adding a single rupee more.
🎢
Volatility 12–16%: how bumpy the ride is
In any given year, your portfolio might swing +30% or −12%. R4 (12.1%) is smoothest. R2 (15.7%) bumpiest. Lower = better sleep.
⚖️
Sharpe ratio: return per unit of risk taken
R4's 0.84 vs R2's 0.62 means R4 gives ~35% more return for the same amount of risk. Better fuel economy for your money.
📉
Max drawdown: worst crash ever
In a 2020-style crash, R4 would drop at most −14.2% from its peak before recovering. R2 could fall −20.7%. The gold ETF is what buffers this.
🔄
Step-Up SIP: the most underrated wealth hack
Raising SIP by just 10%/year (₹10K → ₹11K next year → ₹12.1K the year after…) nearly doubles your wealth over 20 years vs a flat SIP. See the Forecast tab.
📊
Why CAGR changes with holding period
Year 1 CAGR can be anywhere from −30% to +60% — markets are unpredictable short-term. Over 10+ years, the noise averages out and CAGR converges toward the fund's long-run mean (~17%). This is why SIP over long horizons is so powerful: you don't need to time the market.
