what do these numbers actually mean?
📈
Expected Return: 16–17% per year
If you put ₹10,000 today and do nothing, in 10 years it becomes roughly ₹47,000–₹50,000. This is how fast your money grows each year, on average.
🎢
Volatility 12–16%: How bumpy is the ride?
In any given year, your portfolio might go up +30% or down −12%. R4 (12.1%) is the smoothest ride; R2 (15.7%) is the bumpiest. Lower volatility = better sleep at night.
⚖️
Sharpe Ratio: bang for your risk buck
R4's Sharpe of 0.84 vs R2's 0.62 means R4 gives you ~35% more return for every unit of risk you take. It's like getting more fuel per litre of petrol.
📉
Max Drawdown: worst-case crash scenario
If markets crashed like 2020, R4's portfolio would drop at most −14.2% from its peak. R2 could fall −20.7%. Gold ETF is what buffers these crashes.
🏆
Bottom line: R4 wins on every metric
More funds = more diversification = smoother returns. R4 has the highest Sharpe, lowest volatility, smallest crash drop, and the best long-term wealth projection. The only downside: 9 funds to manage instead of 6.
🔄
Step-Up SIP: the biggest wealth multiplier
Increasing your SIP by just 10% every year can nearly double your final wealth over 20 years compared to a flat SIP. See the Forecast tab to compare.
💰
CAGR vs Returns: what's the difference?
CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the steady growth rate that would give you the same final result. Even if one year is +40% and next is −10%, CAGR smooths it to, say, 17%. It's the real measure of how fast wealth compounds.
